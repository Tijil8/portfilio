A little about me.

The short version: I build things, I measure them, and I have strong opinions about Cristiano Ronaldo. The longer version is below.

What I do

These days I'm an engineer at SatSure, working on geospatial data at a scale where the usual rules stop applying — pipelines and systems that take terabytes of raw satellite imagery and turn them into clean, queryable products that teams reach for every day. When the input is a country's worth of pixels, "just process it" becomes a genuinely interesting problem.

Before that, I built a complete job portal for a Netherlands-based company — the whole thing, end to end — a two-sided platform where recruiters and candidates each get their own world. It's live, and people use it.

Outside of work I go deeper than my day job asks me to: hand-rolled systems in C++, games compiled to run in your browser, and experiments where I prove a claim with a measurement instead of an adjective. Most of that lives on the Projects page and in the Lab.
